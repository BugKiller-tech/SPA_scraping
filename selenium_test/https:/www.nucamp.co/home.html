Jane's Story: From Accounting to Web Developement

Between juggling contract bookkeeping work and being a barista, Jane made just enough money to get by. She was grateful for the work, but the sporadic hours and constant commuting between two jobs was taxing.

Her life took an unexpected turn when her accounting firm lost its biggest client and let Jane go. She was able to pick up a few extra shifts at the coffee shop...but she knew it wasn’t sustainable.

Jane thought about going back to school, but it seemed impossible. The cost, the time and the debt was just too much.

A year ago, Jane’s boyfriend encouraged her to think about web development and learning to code. She had watched a few videos and was definitely interested, but life got in the way and video-watching quickly fell off her radar.

Jane had heard about coding bootcamps and was intrigued. Having a dedicated instructor, going to class and learning with others sounded ideal. But, when she looked into it, it was tens of thousands of dollars and required her to quit her job to attend class fulltime. Taking on debt was just not in the cards.

A "nu" Kind of Coding Bootcamp

Jane was scrolling through her Facebook feed and then she saw it: a post about Nucamp. A way to pick up web development skills with a blend of online learning and in-person classes. The cost was far lower than other bootcamps she had seen, she didn’t need to quit her job and it was actually located in her home town. No long commutes needed! She decided to sign up for the front-end program the next day.

Jane quickly realized Nucamp filled all the holes that video watching just didn’t cover. By offering a daily scheduling system and dedicated instructor, and connecting her with other students in her local community, Jane had the ongoing motivation and support she needed to finish her training.

During the bootcamp, Jane was even able to pick up freelance web development jobs. They were small jobs at first, but they gave her necessary work experience. Just recently, she won a year-long contract with a local startup. She’s still learning and building up her coding confidence. But now she gets paid for it while doing a job she loves.
